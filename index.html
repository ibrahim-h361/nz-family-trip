02
Auckland → Hobbiton → Rotorua
Friday, April 3
▼
🌤️ 19°C / 9°C 🌧️ ~40% rain chance Inland Waikato is cooler, especially mornings. Misty farmland at dawn, pleasant afternoons when dry. Rotorua is humid from the geothermal steam.

👚 What to Wear

Layers! Mornings can be single digits. Warm fleece under a waterproof jacket. Long trousers, sturdy walking shoes (Hobbiton paths can be muddy). Hat for sun on clear days. Don't wear your favourites to the geothermal areas — sulphur clings to clothes!

🚗Drive from Auckland to Matamata (~2 hrs)
🧙Hobbiton Movie Set Tour — step into Middle-earth! ~2.5 hrs, includes a drink at The Green Dragon Inn
🌋Continue to Rotorua (~45 min)
♨️Evening at Polynesian Spa — lakefront hot mineral pools, magical after dark
🌙 Overnight in Rotorua

🍽️ Where to Eat

The Shire's Rest Café
Lunch — adjacent to Hobbiton
Convenient pre/post-tour option with light meals and coffee. No booking needed.
The Redoubt Bar & Eatery
Lunch — Matamata town
Great post-Hobbiton debrief spot. Crispy pork belly, fish & chips, chowder. Family-friendly.
Ali Baba's Tunisian Cuisine
Dinner in Rotorua — 100% Halal
Family-owned since 2004. Kebabs, gourmet pizzas, lamb/chicken/falafel mains with salads. Warm family vibe.
Polynesian Spa
Hot Pools — Rotorua Lakefront
28 hot pools fed by two natural springs. Family pool at 33°C with hydro slide, plus adult mineral pools at 37-40°C. Lifeguard-supervised.

03
Rotorua → Auckland
Saturday, April 4
▼
🌫️ 18°C / 9°C 🌧️ ~37% rain chance Misty mornings, high humidity from the geothermal activity. Often overcast early, clearing by midday.

👚 What to Wear

Long-sleeve base layer, fleece mid-layer, waterproof shell. Shoes with grip — geothermal boardwalks can be slippery. Again, wear clothes you don't mind getting sulphur-scented!

🌋Morning at Wai-O-Tapu Thermal Wonderland — the most visually spectacular geothermal park in NZ
💥Lady Knox Geyser erupts daily at 10:15 AM — arrive by 9:45!
🚗Drive back to Auckland (~3 hrs) — scenic countryside route

⭐ Highlights & Tips

Wai-O-Tapu Thermal Wonderland
NZ$45 adult, NZ$15 child • No booking required
Champagne Pool, Devil's Bath, Artist's Palette. 1-1.5 hr boardwalk loop. More photogenic than Te Puia, less crowded. Opens 8:30 AM.
Artisan Café
Brunch in Rotorua
Famous eggs bene, cheesy scones, pancakes, and health-spiked lattes. One of Rotorua's most popular brunch spots.
Eastwood Café
Alt Brunch — forest setting
Beautiful building in the forest within Scion research grounds. Waffles, shakshuka, breakfast pizza. A unique setting.

04
Taupō Adventure + Overnight Stay
Sunday, April 5
▼
🍃 17°C / 7°C 🌧️ ~33% rain chance Noticeably cooler — inland at 370m elevation. Crisp mornings, pleasant midday, cool evenings. Wind off the lake adds chill.

👚 What to Wear

Warm layers! Thermal base layer for early mornings, fleece/wool mid-layer, waterproof windbreaker. Long trousers (not shorts). Warm socks, beanie for lakeside walks. Closed-toe shoes or hiking boots.

🚗Morning drive from Auckland to Taupō (~3.5 hrs) through rolling green hills
💧Visit Huka Falls — 220,000 litres/second of turquoise thunder!
🏞️Great Lake Walkway along Lake Taupō — NZ's largest lake
🍴Dinner in Taupō town — lakefront restaurants with mountain views
🌙 Overnight in Taupō or Rotorua — cosy Airbnb

⭐ Things to Do

Māori Rock Carvings Cruise
~NZ$59/adult • 90 min • Book ahead
14-metre-tall carvings of Ngātoroirangi, only accessible by water. Chris Jolly Outdoors or Sail Barbary (eco electric yacht). Includes muffin + hot drink.
Craters of the Moon
NZ$10/adult • 40 min loop • No booking
Lunar landscape with bubbling craters, steam vents, colourful soils. Easy boardwalk, all ages. Opens 8:30 AM.
Taupō DeBretts Hot Springs
Hot Pools • Family pass available
Outdoor mineral pools, freshwater pool, dragon hydro slide. Open 365 days. Perfect evening wind-down.
Great Lake Walkway
Free • Flat • Any distance
10 km paved lakefront path. Best section: Boat Harbour to Two Mile Bay (~4 km return, ~1 hr). Peaceful bays, hot-water beaches, volcano views.

🍽️ Dinner in Taupō

Edgewater Restaurant
Contemporary NZ • Lakefront
Premier lakefront dining with uninterrupted lake views. Refined seasonal menu. The special-occasion pick. Book ahead.
Dixie Browns
Family-Friendly • Lake views
On the lake shore with views of Ruapehu, Tongariro & Ngauruhoe. Good menu variety, relaxed vibe.
Vine Eatery & Bar
Mediterranean Tapas / NZ Fusion
Local favourite. Tapas meets NZ fine dining. Great for sharing plates as a group.

🏡 Airbnb Stay

Spend the night in a cosy Airbnb in Taupō or Rotorua — no rush to drive back! Wake up to volcanic landscapes and fresh morning air.

05
Waiheke Island Day Trip
Monday, April 6
▼
🌞 20°C / 15°C 🌧️ ~43% rain chance Slightly milder than Auckland thanks to maritime influence. One of the more pleasant spots — mix of sunshine and passing showers.

👚 What to Wear

Light layers — tee/long-sleeve with a light jacket. Packable rain jacket is a must. Comfortable walking shoes for vineyard paths and beaches. Sunglasses & sunscreen (UV still moderate). Cardigan for the ferry ride.

🚗Morning drive back from Taupō/Rotorua to Auckland
⛴️Fullers Ferry to Waiheke Island from downtown Auckland (~40 min scenic ride)
🏖️Explore stunning beaches, olive groves & boutique vineyards
🍽️Long island lunch — fresh seafood with ocean views
⛵Ferry back & evening dinner at Viaduct / Wynyard Quarter

🏖️ Best Beaches

Oneroa Beach
Main beach • Steps from village
Calm, north-facing, ideal for families. Shops and cafes right there. The easiest beach from the ferry.
Palm Beach
Family facilities • BBQ • Playground
Toilets, BBQ, children's playground, picnic areas. All the family amenities. Arcadia Restaurant right on the beach.
Onetangi Beach
Longest beach • Golden sand
Wide golden sand, great for long walks and swimming. Playground and benches along the shore.

🍽️ Lunch on the Island

Tantalus Estate
Vineyard Restaurant
Inventive food, award-winning wines, stunning vineyard setting. The approach alone takes your breath away. Great for a longer, indulgent lunch.
The Heke
Casual Contemporary
Wood-fired pizzas, smoked kahawai, famous smash burger. Relaxed island vibe, perfect for a group.

🚶 Things to Do

Waiheke Art Trail
3 km walk • Free • From ferry terminal
Family-friendly scenic walk through native bush. Art sculptures along the way with sweeping coastal views.
Vineyard Hopping
Walk between vineyards
Many vineyards are clustered and walkable. Waiheke is famous for Syrah and Bordeaux-style reds. Tantalus to Wild on Waiheke is 5 min walk.

Auckland → Queenstown
Tuesday, April 7
▼
❄️ 12°C / 3°C 🌧️ ~37% rain chance Significantly colder than the North Island! Stunning autumn colours but genuinely cold mornings — near freezing. Snow on mountain peaks. Layer up!

👚 What to Wear

Winter-weight layers! Thermal base (top + bottom), wool/fleece mid-layer, insulated waterproof jacket. Warm trousers (jeans alone won't be enough in the evening). Beanie, gloves, scarf for mornings & evenings. A down puffer jacket is ideal. Warm waterproof boots.

✈️Early flight 7:10 → 9:05 AM — soaring over the Southern Alps!
🏔️Arrive in Queenstown — the adventure capital of the world
🚶Afternoon lakeside walk through Queenstown Gardens — 2 km loop along Lake Wakatipu
☕Explore Queenstown town centre, cafés & boutiques
🌙 Night 1 at Queenstown Hotel

🍽️ Where to Eat

Fergburger
Burgers • Iconic!
The most famous burger joint in NZ. Expect a queue but it moves fast. Massive, indulgent burgers. A Queenstown rite of passage.
Public Kitchen
Tapas / Sharing • Lakeside
On Steamer Wharf with lake views. Tapas-style sharing plates — perfect for a group dinner on the first night.
The Cow
Pizza & Pasta • Family favourite
Stone walls, open fire, family favourite for 40+ years. Cosy, rustic, and delicious.
Patagonia Chocolaterie
Dessert • Don't miss!
Best chocolate and desserts in Queenstown. Perfect post-dinner stop for the family. Hot chocolate is legendary.

07
Milford Sound Day Trip
Wednesday, April 8
▼
🌧️ 14°C / 7°C 🌧️ ~50% rain chance — 548mm in April alone! One of the wettest places on Earth (~7 metres of rain/year). But rain here is GOOD — it creates hundreds of temporary waterfalls cascading down the cliffs. Iconic!

👚 What to Wear — IMPORTANT!

Full waterproof gear is non-negotiable! Waterproof jacket AND waterproof over-trousers. Waterproof footwear (not just water-resistant). Thermals + fleece underneath. Bring a dry bag / zip-lock bags for phone, camera & wallet. Pack a complete change of dry clothes for after.

🚌Early morning coach pickup from Queenstown (~7 AM)
🏔️4-hour scenic drive through ancient rainforest, Mirror Lakes, Homer Tunnel
🛳️Milford Sound cruise — Mitre Peak, waterfalls, dolphins & seals!
🌃Late return to Queenstown (~7-8 PM)
🌙 Night 2 at Queenstown Hotel

🛳️ Which Cruise to Book

RealNZ Signature Cruise
~NZ$130-285 • ~2 hrs • Book well ahead!
93% recommended on TripAdvisor. Barista coffee, local craft beer, fresh cabinet food. Day trip from QT includes coach + cruise (~12-14 hrs total).
Cruise Milford (Boutique)
Max 80 guests • 2,300+ five-star reviews
More intimate experience. Includes champagne and snacks. Gets closer to waterfalls and wildlife. Premium pick.

📷 Scenic Stops on the Drive

Te Anau, Mirror Lakes, Homer Tunnel
Included in coach tour
Coffee stop in Te Anau, photo stops at Mirror Lakes and Monkey Creek, then the dramatic 1.2 km Homer Tunnel carved through the mountain.

Queenstown → Auckland
Thursday, April 9
▼
❄️ 12°C / 3°C 🌧️ ~37% rain chance Same crisp Queenstown autumn. Frosty mornings, clear skies make for spectacular gondola views. Wrap up warm for the summit!

👚 What to Wear

Same winter layers as Day 6. Extra warm for the Gondola summit — it's windier and colder at the top! Beanie & gloves essential for the morning lakeside walk.

🚶Morning lakeside walk & brunch by the water
🚠Optional: Skyline Gondola — steepest cable car in the Southern Hemisphere + Luge!
🚢Optional: TSS Earnslaw vintage 1912 coal-fired steamship cruise
✈️Afternoon flight 5:30 → 7:20 PM back to Auckland

🍽️ Brunch Spots

Bespoke Kitchen
Award-winning brunch • 9 Isle St
Multi-award-winning. Blackberry spelt pancakes, vegan benedict, incredible cabinet food. Healthy-leaning, just below the Gondola entrance.
Yonder
Creative brunch • 14 Church St
"Best coffee in Queenstown." Passionfruit & cheesecake French toast, boysenberry pancakes. Popular with locals.
Vudu Café & Larder
Lakeside brunch
Sit right by the lake. Classic brunch fare, good coffee, scenic and central. Can't beat the view.

⭐ Activities

Skyline Gondola + Luge
Family of 4: ~NZ$179 • No booking needed
Rise 480m to Bob's Peak. Jaw-dropping 360° views. Luge: 1,600m of banked corners & tunnels (~15 min/ride). Kids 6+ ride solo, 2+ ride tandem.
TSS Earnslaw Steamship
90 min cruise • Book at realnz.com
One of the last coal-fired steamships in the world (1912!). Watch stokers shovel coal. Option to add Gourmet BBQ Lunch at Walter Peak Farm.

Auckland Scenic Buffer Day
Friday, April 10
▼
⛅ 20°C / 13°C 🌧️ ~45% rain chance Back to mild Auckland autumn. After Queenstown's chill, this will feel positively warm! Changeable skies, rain jacket at the ready.

👚 What to Wear

Back to Auckland layers — tee + jacket, rain jacket in the bag. Comfortable walking shoes for Mount Eden & the museum. Light scarf for the Devonport ferry if you go.

😌Flexible recovery day — sleep in, take it easy!
🏛️Auckland Domain & War Memorial Museum
⛰️Mount Eden — 360° views of the city & harbour
⛴️Optional: Devonport ferry for a charming seaside village
🍽️Farewell dinner at Viaduct / Wynyard Quarter

⭐ Things to Do

Auckland War Memorial Museum
NZ$27 adult, NZ$13.50 child • 2-3 hrs
Three floors: Māori culture, NZ natural history, war memorials. The Māori Cultural Show is the must-see. Set in beautiful Auckland Domain parklands.
Mount Eden (Maungawhau)
Free • ~20 min walk to summit
Auckland's highest volcano (196m). 50-metre-deep crater at the summit. 360° panoramic views. Cars banned — walk only. Lovely at sunset!
Devonport Village
12 min ferry • ~NZ$11.50 each way
Charming seaside village. Walk up Mt Victoria for skyline views, explore North Head military tunnels, browse Victoria Road boutiques & cafes.

🍽️ Farewell Dinner

Soul Bar & Bistro (Viaduct)
Bookend the trip!
Return to the Viaduct for the last night. Consistently excellent for a celebratory farewell meal by the harbour.
Bawarchi Restaurant
Indian • Halal-certified
Vibrant atmosphere, aromatic biryanis, tandoori, and curries. Good halal option for a warm, flavourful farewell dinner.
