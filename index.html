Wednesday, April 8
▼
Cruising Milford Sound
Mitre Peak rising from the fjord
Waterfalls cascading into the fjord
🌧️14°C / 7°C🌧️ ~50% — 548mm in April alone!One of the wettest places on Earth! But rain creates hundreds of temporary waterfalls. It's actually MORE spectacular in the rain!

👚 What to Wear — IMPORTANT!

Full waterproof gear! Jacket AND over-trousers. Waterproof footwear. Thermals + fleece. Dry bags for phone/camera. Pack a complete change of dry clothes.

🚌Coach pickup ~7 AM from Queenstown
🏔️4-hour scenic drive — Mirror Lakes, Homer Tunnel
🛳️Milford Sound cruise — Mitre Peak, waterfalls, dolphins & seals!
🌃Late return ~7-8 PM
🌙 Night 2 at Queenstown Hotel
💡 Fun Fact
Milford Sound is technically a fjord, not a sound! Captain Cook named it wrong. It was carved by glaciers, not rivers. Mitre Peak rises 1,692m almost vertically from the water.
